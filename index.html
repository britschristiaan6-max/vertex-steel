/ In the shop

From plate to patio

Every piece runs the same four stations. No castings, no imported shells — solid steel, worked by hand.

01

Cut

Your whole pit is plasma-cut from a single sheet of mild steel plate — squares, triangles, whatever the design calls for. Clean edges, tight nesting.

02

Form

Panels are folded and press-braked into the final shape. This is where a flat sheet becomes something that holds a fire.

03

Weld

Every seam is MIG-welded solid and ground flush — joints built to take real heat and outlast the guarantee.

04

Finish

Mild steel is wire-brushed and oil-sealed; stainless is brushed bright. Then crated and couriered to your door.

/ The steel & the fire

Pick your steel

The metal you choose changes the look, the aging, and the price. Here's the honest rundown — and how it burns.

Mild · 1.2–3 mm

Mild Steel

Our workhorse plate. Wire-brushed and oil-sealed to a deep gunmetal that darkens with every fire — ages like a good cast-iron pan. Best value.

304 · 1.2–3 mm

Stainless Steel

Bright, rust-proof, and low-maintenance. The premium pick for coastal air, poolsides, and clean modern builds. Wipes clean, won't stain.

Fuel · No gas

Wood & Charcoal

Built for real fire — wood and charcoal, not a gas burner. Proper coals, proper flame, a proper braai. The heat and the smell you actually want.
